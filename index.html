Mega G Technologies

Cleantech Generator Manufacturing

Mega G Technologies developed an innovative cleantech generator that converts mechanical energy into electricity with 150% greater efficiency than conventional models while producing zero emissions.

Mpho Moloko has developed a 3D-printed renewable energy generator aimed at helping solve South Africa's energy crisis. The generator, which does not use fossil fuels, is designed for homes, businesses, and public facilities like hospitals. Moloko, who studied mechanical and electrical engineering at Taletso TVET College, built the prototype in 2021 with support from the Mafikeng Digital Innovation Hub. He gained his 3D printing skills through ALISON, an online learning platform. Although still a prototype, the generator delivers over 150% efficiency and could power electric vehicles. Moloko hopes to become an Independent Power Producer to offer affordable electricity to underserved communities and reduce load-shedding in the next decade.

Their solution addresses both energy poverty and climate change, particularly benefiting rural communities with limited grid access.

Mathabi's Noise to Energy Project

Urban Energy Harvesting

Mathabi, a young innovator from Mahikeng, created a system that converts noise pollution from busy roads and industrial areas into usable electrical energy.

"Through GCIP's mentorship, we improved our prototype's efficiency by 65% and secured our first municipal pilot project." - Mathabi, Founder

The technology has potential applications in urban areas across South Africa, turning environmental noise pollution into a valuable energy resource while reducing its health impacts.
